Your photo's story, told the same way every time

Most field tools stamp a date and a location onto the corner of your photo. That's a caption. A caption can be cropped, edited, or argued away.

RaizIntel does something different. The moment you capture a photo, it captures everything around it — the GPS location, the exact time, the device it came from — and binds it to a permanent, add-only record of custody. Nothing in that record can be quietly changed after the fact. If a photo was taken at 4:12 PM at a specific address on a specific phone, the record says so — in the field, in your report, and on the stand.

You get a clean chain from shutter to courtroom, without keeping a separate log or hoping your memory holds up months later.

  • Metadata at the source

    GPS, timestamp, and device details are captured with the photo, not typed in afterward.

  • An add-only custody record

    Every photo is logged the moment it's taken. Entries can be added, never rewritten or erased.

  • Made to be questioned

    When opposing counsel asks how you know, the answer is already in the file.
